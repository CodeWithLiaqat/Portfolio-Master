Vary sentence length. Take a position in each article — a real point of view is what generic AI text never has. Read each article aloud-in-your-head before committing; rewrite any sentence you've seen on a hundred agency blogs.

## 3 · SITEWIDE TEXT-OVER-IMAGE READABILITY (audit every section)

The Home hero keeps its bold treatment. Everywhere else, enforce this **readability stack** wherever text sits over imagery or the canvas:

1. Text never sits directly on busy imagery. Either the media drops behind a scrim, or the text sits on a panel.
2. **Scrim recipe (standard):** gradient overlay `linear-gradient(to top, color-mix(in oklab, var(--bg-0) 92%, transparent), color-mix(in oklab, var(--bg-0) 35%, transparent) 60%, transparent)` oriented toward the text block; media opacity capped at 0.35 behind body text.
3. **Panel recipe (when the image should stay vivid):** the house glass surface behind the text block, with 12–20px breathing padding.
4. Verify computed contrast after overlays: body ≥7:1, large display ≥4.5:1, in **all three themes** (Garnet is the risk case — check it explicitly).
5. Audit route by route — every section of every page — and list in your report which sections you adjusted and which recipe you applied. Images should read as atmosphere that supports the words, never as competition.
